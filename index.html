Frequently asked questions

Helpful answers about gas compliance, safety, and installation.

Quick answers to the questions property owners ask most before an installation, inspection, or compliance check.

What is a CoC?

A Gas Certificate of Compliance confirms that a gas installation has been inspected and meets the required safety regulations.

Why do you need a CoC?

You may need a CoC for property sales, insurance requirements, and proof that the installation is legally compliant and safe.

How long is a CoC valid?

A CoC remains valid as long as no changes are made to the gas installation and the system remains in compliant condition.

How often must a gas installation be serviced?

Gas appliances such as geysers, fireplaces, and braais should be serviced yearly for safe operation and optimal performance.

Can a gas cylinder be installed inside a house?

Cylinder placement depends on the appliance type, size, and the applicable regulations. We assess every property to ensure safe positioning.

Where can a gas cylinder be installed outside?

Outdoor cylinders must be installed in approved, ventilated locations with the correct clearances from doors, drains, and ignition sources.

What do I do if I suspect a gas leak?

Turn off the gas supply if safe to do so, avoid flames or switches, ventilate the area, and call a qualified gas professional immediately.
